## Overview

The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) Momentum Strategy is a sophisticated technical analysis approach for capturing mid-term trend accelerations over 3-6 week periods. This strategy uses momentum shifts and signal line crossovers to identify optimal entry and exit points in trending markets.

## Strategy Components

### MACD Indicator Explained

MACD consists of three components:
- **MACD Line**: 12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA (fast line)
- **Signal Line**: 9-day EMA of MACD line (slow line)
- **Histogram**: Visual representation of the distance between MACD and signal line

### Setup Parameters

- **MACD Settings**: 12, 26, 9 (standard parameters)
- **Timeframe**: Daily charts for signals, weekly for trend context
- **Target Holding Period**: 3-6 weeks
- **Additional Filters**: Volume, trend alignment, RSI confirmation

## Implementation Strategy

### Primary Entry Signals

**Bullish Signal (MACD Crossover)**:
1. MACD line crosses above signal line (bullish crossover)
2. MACD histogram turns positive (green bars)
3. MACD line is above zero line (bullish momentum)
4. Price is above 50-day moving average (trend confirmation)
5. Volume increases on crossover day

**Bearish Signal (Short Setup)**:
1. MACD line crosses below signal line (bearish crossover)
2. MACD histogram turns negative (red bars)
3. MACD line is below zero line (bearish momentum)
4. Price is below 50-day moving average
5. Volume increases on crossover

### Entry Rules

**Long Entry**:
- Enter on the day of bullish crossover or next day
- Preferred: Enter on pullback to 20-day EMA after crossover
- Confirm with RSI above 50 (momentum confirmation)
- Stop loss: Below recent swing low or 6-8% below entry

**Short Entry**:
- Enter on the day of bearish crossover or next day
- Preferred: Enter on bounce to 20-day EMA after crossover
- Confirm with RSI below 50
- Stop loss: Above recent swing high or 6-8% above entry

### Exit Rules

1. **Target 1**: 10-12% profit (take 50% position)
2. **Target 2**: 15-18% profit (take remaining 50%)
3. Exit on opposite MACD crossover signal
4. Exit if MACD histogram shows diminishing momentum (divergence)
5. Exit if price crosses back below (long) or above (short) the 50-day MA
6. Trail stop to breakeven after 7% gain

## Advanced MACD Patterns

### MACD Divergence

**Bullish Divergence**:
- Price makes lower low, MACD makes higher low
- Signals potential upward reversal
- Enter on MACD crossover confirmation

**Bearish Divergence**:
- Price makes higher high, MACD makes lower high
- Signals potential downward reversal
- Enter on MACD crossover confirmation

### MACD Histogram Patterns

- **Increasing Histogram**: Strong momentum (hold position)
- **Decreasing Histogram**: Momentum weakening (consider scaling out)
- **Histogram Crossover Zero**: Momentum shift (potential reversal)

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis

1. **Weekly MACD**: Confirm overall trend direction
2. **Daily MACD**: Generate entry signals
3. **Hourly MACD**: Fine-tune entry timing

**Rule**: Only trade daily signals that align with weekly trend (increases win rate by 20-25%)

## Risk Management

- **Position Size**: 2% of portfolio per trade
- **Maximum Concurrent Positions**: 5 trades
- **Momentum Filter**: Only trade when MACD is above/below zero line
- **Trend Filter**: Only trade in direction of 50-day MA slope
- **Drawdown Control**: Reduce position size after 3 consecutive losses

## Confirmation Indicators

Combine MACD with:
- **Volume**: Increasing volume on crossover signals
- **RSI**: RSI >50 for longs, <50 for shorts
- **Moving Averages**: Price above/below key MAs
- **Support/Resistance**: Crossovers near key levels
- **Market Context**: Sector and market trend alignment

## Strengths

- Excellent at identifying trend changes early
- Combines trend and momentum analysis
- Works well with other indicators
- Clear visual signals (histogram)
- Adaptable to various timeframes

## Limitations

- Lagging indicator (signals come after move starts)
- Whipsaws in sideways markets
- Requires trend for optimal performance
- Can give false signals in choppy conditions

## Market Conditions

**Optimal Conditions**:
- Clear trending markets (up or down)
- Moderate to high volatility
- Strong momentum shifts
- Aligned sector trends

**Avoid Using When**:
- Choppy, sideways markets
- Low volatility environments
- Conflicting signals across timeframes
- During major news events (wait for clarity)

## Strategy Variations

### Conservative Approach
- Only trade MACD crossovers above/below zero line
- Require 50-day MA alignment
- Add RSI confirmation (>60 for longs, <40 for shorts)
- Trade only with weekly trend alignment

### Aggressive Approach
- Trade all MACD crossovers
- Use histogram divergences as early signals
- Multiple timeframe entries
- Tighter stops (4-5%)

### Hybrid Approach
- Primary signals from MACD crossovers
- Add breakout confirmation from key levels
- Use volume spikes for validation
- Combine with support/resistance analysis
