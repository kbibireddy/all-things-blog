## Overview

The Support and Resistance Breakout Strategy is a foundational technical analysis approach for mid-term trading (3-6 weeks). This strategy focuses on identifying and trading breakouts from established price levels where supply and demand have historically created barriers.

## Strategy Components

### Key Concepts

**Support Level**: A price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further price decline. Often formed at previous lows, round numbers, or significant volume nodes.

**Resistance Level**: A price level where selling pressure prevents further price advance. Typically formed at previous highs, round numbers, or areas of high volume selling.

**Breakout**: When price moves decisively above resistance or below support, often accompanied by increased volume, indicating a potential continuation of the new trend.

## Setup Parameters

- **Timeframe**: Daily charts for level identification and weekly for trend context
- **Level Strength**: Minimum 3 touches for strong support/resistance
- **Volume Confirmation**: At least 1.5x average volume on breakout
- **Holding Period**: 3-6 weeks from breakout
- **Price Action**: Clean breakouts with minimal wicks beyond the level

## Implementation Strategy

### Identifying Key Levels

1. **Horizontal Support/Resistance**:
   - Previous swing highs and lows
   - Price levels with multiple touches (3+)
   - Levels where price paused or reversed

2. **Trend Lines**:
   - Upward trend lines connecting higher lows (support)
   - Downward trend lines connecting lower highs (resistance)

3. **Dynamic Levels**:
   - Key moving averages (50-day, 200-day)
   - Fibonacci retracement levels (38.2%, 50%, 61.8%)
   - Volume profile value areas

### Entry Rules for Breakout Above Resistance

1. Identify strong resistance level (minimum 3 touches, 20+ days)
2. Wait for price to close above resistance with increased volume (1.5x+ average)
3. Confirm with follow-through (next 2-3 days hold above breakout level)
4. Enter on pullback to resistance (now support) or on breakout if strong momentum
5. Alternative: Enter on retest and bounce from breakout level

**Stop Loss**: Below the breakout level (2-3% below for tight stops, 5-7% for wider stops)

### Entry Rules for Breakdown Below Support

1. Identify strong support level (minimum 3 touches, 20+ days)
2. Wait for price to close below support with increased volume (1.5x+ average)
3. Confirm breakdown holds for 2-3 days
4. Enter on bounce to support (now resistance) or on breakdown if strong momentum
5. Alternative: Enter on retest and rejection from breakdown level

**Stop Loss**: Above the breakdown level (2-3% above)

### Exit Rules

1. **Target 1**: Measure move target (distance from level to opposite extreme = target)
   - Take 50% profit at 8-10% gain
2. **Target 2**: Extended target (1.5x measure move)
   - Take remaining 50% at 12-15% gain
3. Exit if price returns below (breakout) or above (breakdown) the key level
4. Exit on opposite breakout signal
5. Trail stop to breakeven after 6% gain

## Breakout Validation Checklist

Before entering, confirm:
- ✓ Volume surge (1.5x minimum, 2x+ preferred)
- ✓ Clean close above/below level (minimal wicks)
- ✓ Level has been tested multiple times (3+)
- ✓ Follow-through for 2-3 days
- ✓ Strong market/sector context
- ✓ No major resistance/support immediately ahead

## Risk Management

- **Position Size**: 2% of portfolio per trade
- **Level Quality**: Stronger levels allow slightly larger positions (up to 2.5%)
- **False Breakouts**: Exit immediately if price returns below/above level
- **Maximum Positions**: 4-5 simultaneous trades
- **Correlation**: Diversify across sectors

## Advanced Techniques

### Volume Profile Analysis

- Identify high-volume nodes (value areas)
- These often act as strong support/resistance
- Breakouts from low-volume nodes are more reliable

### Multiple Timeframe Confirmation

1. Identify level on daily chart
2. Confirm trend on weekly chart (breakout should align with weekly trend)
3. Use hourly chart for precise entry timing
4. Weekly alignment increases success rate significantly

### Breakout Patterns

- **Cup and Handle**: Bullish continuation pattern
- **Ascending Triangle**: Bullish breakout pattern
- **Descending Triangle**: Bearish breakdown pattern
- **Rectangle/Range**: Breakout in direction of prevailing trend

## Practical Example

**Breakout Setup**:
1. Stock trading between $45 (support) and $55 (resistance) for 3 months
2. Resistance tested 4 times, support tested 3 times
3. Price breaks above $55 on 2x average volume
4. Next 2 days hold above $55 (follow-through confirmed)
5. Enter on pullback to $55.50 (resistance now support)
6. Stop loss at $53.50 (below breakout level)
7. Measure move: $55 - $45 = $10, so target 1 at $65 (50% off), target 2 at $70 (remaining 50%)
