## Overview

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) Divergence Strategy is a powerful technique for identifying potential trend reversals in the 3-6 week timeframe. This strategy focuses on detecting discrepancies between price action and momentum, providing early signals for mid-term position entries.

## Strategy Components

### Setup Parameters

- **RSI Period**: 14-day (standard)
- **Timeframe**: Daily charts for entry, weekly for trend confirmation
- **RSI Levels**: Oversold (<30), Neutral (30-70), Overbought (>70)
- **Target Holding Period**: 3-6 weeks

### Divergence Types

**Bullish Divergence**:
- Price makes lower lows while RSI makes higher lows
- Signals potential upward reversal
- Most reliable when RSI is in oversold territory (<30)

**Bearish Divergence**:
- Price makes higher highs while RSI makes lower highs
- Signals potential downward reversal
- Most reliable when RSI is in overbought territory (>70)

## Implementation Strategy

### Entry Rules for Bullish Divergence

1. Identify lower low in price with higher low in RSI
2. RSI should be below 40 (preferably below 30)
3. Wait for price to break above the most recent swing high
4. Confirm with volume increase on breakout
5. Entry: On breakout or pullback to breakout level

**Stop Loss**: Below the most recent swing low (or 6-8% below entry)

### Entry Rules for Bearish Divergence (Shorting)

1. Identify higher high in price with lower high in RSI
2. RSI should be above 60 (preferably above 70)
3. Wait for price to break below the most recent swing low
4. Confirm with volume increase on breakdown
5. Entry: On breakdown or pullback to breakdown level

**Stop Loss**: Above the most recent swing high (or 6-8% above entry)

### Exit Rules

1. **Target 1**: 10-12% profit (take 50% off)
2. **Target 2**: 15-18% profit (take remaining 50%)
3. Exit on opposite divergence signal
4. Exit if RSI reaches extreme opposite level (>80 or <20)
5. Trail stop to breakeven after 8% gain

## Advanced Techniques

### Confirmation Indicators

- **Volume**: Increasing volume on breakout/breakdown
- **MACD**: MACD histogram showing momentum shift
- **Moving Averages**: Price breaking key MA levels
- **Support/Resistance**: Divergence occurring at key levels

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis

1. Identify divergence on daily chart
2. Confirm trend direction on weekly chart
3. Use hourly chart for precise entry timing
4. Weekly trend alignment increases success rate by 15-20%

## Risk Management

- **Position Size**: 1.5-2% of portfolio per trade (divergence can be early)
- **Maximum Positions**: 4-5 simultaneous trades
- **Correlation**: Avoid multiple positions in highly correlated sectors
- **Drawdown Control**: Reduce position size after 2 consecutive losses

- Requires subjective pattern recognition
- False signals more common in strong trends

## Market Conditions

**Best Performance**:
- End of strong trends (reversal setup)
- Range-bound markets
- Markets showing exhaustion signs
- Oversold/overbought extremes

**Avoid Using When**:
- Strong momentum trends (divergence can persist)
- Low volatility environments
- Illiquid stocks with erratic RSI
- During major news events
